Abstract Impressionism

Abstract expressionism is an artistic movement occuring during the 1940s and 50s that bridged the gap between modern and postmodern art. Pollock and De Kooning abandoned the role of subject and traditional painting techniques altogether during this time, giving birth to a new genre of expressionism.

Pop Art

Pop art incorporates mainstream and pop culture imagery to create a fine art movement that challenged the notions of art itself. The consumer good and celebrity subject matter seems like a tired aesthetic, but at the time challenged the conception of fine art, blurring the line between it and low-brow art.

Basquiat built upon the ideals of abstract expressionism and pop art to create postmodern art. His new wave of neo-expressionism contained elements of low-brow street art and saw the return of cryptic traditional figures and symbols while still maintaining the visceral nature of abstract expressionism.
